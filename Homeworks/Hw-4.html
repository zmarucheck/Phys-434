Problem 2¶

Part 2a) Make a 2d histogram plot. Read about options, and play with binsize so it is useful (too large a bin and everything washes out, too small and the sample noise dominates). Make sure you use a colorbar.¶

In [62]:
import matplotlib.colors as colors

x = stats.norm.rvs(size=int(1e5))*np.sqrt(N)+N
y = stats.norm.rvs(size=int(1e5))*np.sqrt(N)+N
fig, ax = plt.subplots()
hh = ax.hist2d(x, y, bins=60, norm=colors.LogNorm())
fig.colorbar(hh[3], ax=ax)
plt.title("2D Histogram of 100k Points Normal vs Normal")
plt.xlabel("X Values")
plt.ylabel("Y Values")
plt.show()

Part 2b) Clearly explain what is being plotted in your plot (e.g. what does each color mean?)¶

The colors represent the density of the data found at that particular x and y segment, with darker blue colors representing areas with few to no points of data, lighter blue to green representing areas with middling amounts of data (~10 to ~100 data points per 2D segment), and yellow representing the areas with the highest density of data (>~120).
